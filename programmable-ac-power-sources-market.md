<p><p>Programmable AC power sources come in two main types: Series mode and Parallel mode. Series mode power sources are configured to increase the voltage output range, allowing users to reach higher voltage levels by stacking multiple units together. In contrast, Parallel mode power sources are designed to increase the current output range, enabling users to achieve higher current levels by connecting multiple units in parallel. Both modes offer flexibility in voltage and current adjustments to meet the specific power requirements of various applications.</p></p>
