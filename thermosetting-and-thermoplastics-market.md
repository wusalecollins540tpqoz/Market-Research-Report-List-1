<p><p>Thermosetting plastics, also known as thermosets, are polymers that irreversibly harden when heated, forming a strong and durable cross-linked structure. They are commonly used in automotive and electrical industries due to their high temperature resistance and excellent mechanical properties. On the other hand, thermoplastics are polymers that can be melted and reshaped multiple times without losing their properties. They are widely used in packaging, consumer goods, and construction industries due to their ease of processing and recyclability.</p><p>The Thermosetting and Thermoplastics Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.8% during the forecast period.
