<p><p>A single-stage torque converter consists of a single set of turbines and stators, while a multistage torque converter has multiple sets. Single-stage converters are simpler and cost-effective, suitable for light-duty vehicles. On the other hand, multistage converters provide higher torque multiplication and efficiency, making them ideal for heavy-duty vehicles such as trucks and buses. Both types improve the efficiency of an automobile's transmission system by transferring power smoothly from the engine to the wheels.</p></p>
