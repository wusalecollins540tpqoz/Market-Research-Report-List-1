<p><p>Amphoteric surfactants are versatile compounds that can act as both anionic and cationic surfactants depending on the pH of the solution. The main types include Betaine, Amine oxide, Amphoacetates, Amphopropionates, and Sultaines. Betaine surfactants are derived from sugar beets and are known for their mildness and conditioning properties. Amine oxides are commonly used as foam stabilizers and viscosity modifiers. Amphoacetates and amphopropionates are gentle surfactants used in personal care products. Sultaines are highly effective at reducing irritation and can act as thickening agents in formulations.</p></p>
