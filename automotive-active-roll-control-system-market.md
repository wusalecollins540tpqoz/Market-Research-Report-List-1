<p><p>Automotive Active Roll Control System refers to a technology that helps in reducing the body roll of a vehicle during cornering or maneuvering. The market for this system is divided into two primary types, namely Electric Active Roll Control (EARC) and Hydraulic Active Roll Control (HARC). EARC utilizes electric motors to generate opposing forces that counteract the body roll, enhancing stability and handling. On the other hand, HARC utilizes hydraulic actuators to control and adjust the stiffness of the anti-roll bars, providing similar benefits. Both systems aim to improve the overall driving experience and safety of vehicles.</p></p>
