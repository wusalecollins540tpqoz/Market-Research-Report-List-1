<p><strong>The Automotive Torque Vectoring System Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Type I</li><li>Type II</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Type I market in the automotive torque vectoring system refers to systems that use the brakes to individually control the speed of each wheel, improving traction and stability during cornering. These systems are typically less expensive but may not offer as precise control. </p><p>On the other hand, Type II market refers to systems that use an additional drivetrain component, such as a differential or electromagnetic clutch, to actively distribute torque between the wheels. This allows for more precise and efficient control, especially in high-performance vehicles.</p></p>
