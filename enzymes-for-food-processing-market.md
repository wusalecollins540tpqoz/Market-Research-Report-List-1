<p><p>Enzymes for food processing can be classified into six types based on their functions: Oxidoreductases, Transferases, Hydrolases, Isomerases, Lyases, and Ligases. Oxidoreductases help in oxidation and reduction reactions, Transferases transfer functional groups between molecules, Hydrolases break down proteins, fats, and carbohydrates, Isomerases change the molecular structure of a compound, Lyases cleave chemical bonds by eliminating groups, and Ligases join two molecules together. Each type of enzyme plays a crucial role in food processing by catalyzing specific reactions to improve the quality and safety of food products.</p></p>
