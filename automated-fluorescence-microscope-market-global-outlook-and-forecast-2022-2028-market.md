<p><p>The global automated fluorescence microscope market is projected to experience substantial growth from 2022 to 2028. It can be categorized into different types including wide-field epifluorescence microscopy, confocal fluorescence microscopy, and total internal reflection fluorescence microscopy (TIRF). Wide-field epifluorescence microscopy involves illuminating the entire sample with UV light and capturing the emitted fluorescence. Confocal fluorescence microscopy improves image resolution by eliminating out-of-focus light. Total internal reflection fluorescence microscopy (TIRF) uses an evanescent wave to excite fluorophores present near the interface of a sample, enabling high resolution imaging of structures close to the surface.</p></p>
