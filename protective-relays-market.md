<p><p>Protective relays are devices used to detect abnormal conditions in electrical systems and initiate a response to prevent damage or dangerous situations. There are three main types of protective relays: electromagnetic relay, static relay, and mechanical relay. Electromagnetic relays operate on the principle of electromagnetism, static relays use solid-state components for sensing and logic functions, and mechanical relays utilize mechanical moving parts to control the circuit. Each type has its own advantages and disadvantages, catering to different applications in the market.</p></p>
